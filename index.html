Inference Engine

Cactus Compute v1.14

C++ inference engine compiled to ARM64. Zero-copy memory mapping, INT4 quantization, Apple Neural Engine acceleration. Same engine runs on phone, tablet, and laptop.

cactuscompute.com

Language Model

Gemma 4 E2B (Google DeepMind)

2.3B effective parameters. 1.5 GB at INT4. Native audio, native vision, and function calling in a single model. The first Apache 2.0 open-source model that enables real edge compute. Launched April 2, 2026 — 17 days ago.

ai.google.dev/gemma

Speech-to-Text

NVIDIA Parakeet TDT 0.6B

4.65% WER — lowest error rate of any on-device speech model. Runs as a fast first pass before the LLM. Apple NPU accelerated for sub-second transcription.

The Pipeline

01

Audio Capture

G2 glasses mic captures PCM audio (16kHz, 16-bit, mono) and streams it over BLE to the iPhone. The Even Realities app relays audio frames to our web app running in its WebView.

even_hub_sdk → audioEvent.audioPcm (Uint8Array)
02

Speech Recognition

3-second audio buffer is sent via HTTP POST to the Marginalia app's local server (localhost:8080). Parakeet TDT transcribes the audio to text with 95.35% accuracy.

cactusTranscribe(sttModel, pcmData) → "I've been feeling burned out..."
03

Tactical Analysis

Gemma 4 E2B receives the transcript with a system prompt containing the scenario context (role, relationship, organizational dynamics). It generates exactly 3 response options with optional function calls.

cactusComplete(llmModel, messages, tools) → {"options": [...]}
04

Lens Display

Options are rendered on the G2 lens via textContainerUpgrade — flicker-free in-place text updates. Ring rotation moves the selection indicator. Ring click triggers the function call.

bridge.textContainerUpgrade() → ◉ Approve ○ Counter ○ Defer
05

Function Execution

When the wearer taps the R1 ring, the selected option's function call executes on-device. For this demo: createCalendarHold(title, startDate, endDate). A calendar event appears on the iPhone screen.

POST /tool-call-json → {"status": "created", "event": {...}}

Why On-Device Changes Everything

Cloud AI can't serve regulated industries. Attorney-client privilege, HIPAA, material non-public information, classified environments — these conversations cannot leave the room. Marginalia is the only AI that's legally deployable where it matters most.

Privileged Training Data

Each vertical gets a fine-tuned model trained on domain-specific corpora under BAA. Our models know what a deposition sounds like, what a differential diagnosis workflow looks like. Cloud LLMs trained on the open internet never will.

On-Device Personalization

Your model learns your voice, your style, your vocabulary over months of use — and that adaptation never leaves your hardware. Six months of personalization is six months of switching cost no competitor can replicate.

Compliance as Moat

SOC 2, HIPAA BAA, HITRUST, FedRAMP. Each certification takes 6-12 months and $100K-500K. Every one we earn is a barrier to entry. We're the only product a Fortune 500 procurement committee can legally approve.

Why Now

The model exists

Gemma 4 E2B shipped April 2, 2026 — the first open-weight model that reasons directly over raw audio on a phone. Before this, on-device voice AI meant chaining STT + LLM + TTS. Now it's one model, one forward pass.

The hardware exists

Smart glasses crossed the line from prototype to product in 2025. The G2 has a mic, a lens, and a ring — and looks like regular glasses. The form factor is finally invisible enough for a boardroom.

The regulations tightened

Executive Order 14110 (AI safety), updated HIPAA enforcement, and post-2024 attorney-client privilege rulings all made cloud AI in sensitive environments legally radioactive. The market need isn't hypothetical — it's compliance-driven demand.
